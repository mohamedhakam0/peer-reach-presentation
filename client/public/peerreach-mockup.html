Peer Reach

no internet · no towers · just people
Works without internet
Peer Reach creates its own network between devices. No Wi-Fi, no cellular signal, no data plan required. It works in remote areas, during outages, or anywhere infrastructure is unavailable.
Offline-first
No SIM needed
No account
BLE mesh networking
Your phone broadcasts encrypted packets over Bluetooth Low Energy. Messages hop from device to device — each node relays what it receives, extending the range of the network automatically.
BLE 5.0
LE Coded PHY
Multi-hop relay
LoRa extends your reach
ESP32 relay nodes bridge BLE clusters over LoRa radio, stretching the network up to 1.4 km. Your message travels BLE → ESP32 → LoRa → ESP32 → BLE to reach peers far beyond Bluetooth range.
868 MHz
~1.4 km range
LR1120
End-to-end encrypted
Every message is encrypted with AES-128-GCM using keys only you and your contact hold. Keys are exchanged in person via QR code — relay nodes forward encrypted blobs they cannot read.
Curve25519
AES-128-GCM
QR key exchange
Fully decentralised
There is no server, no account, no authority. Every device is equal. The network forms itself from whoever is nearby. If a node disappears, messages reroute automatically through other peers.
No server
Self-forming
Resilient routing
